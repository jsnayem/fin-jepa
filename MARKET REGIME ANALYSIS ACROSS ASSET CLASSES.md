MARKET REGIME ANALYSIS ACROSS ASSET CLASSES: A UMAP CLUSTERING STUDY OF CRYPTOCURRENCY, FOREX, AND EQUITY MARKETS IGOR RIVIN Abstract. We present a comprehensive analysis of market regime structures across four major asset classes using UMAP][MHM18] dimensionality reduction and K-means clustering on intra- day price patterns (2021-2024). Our analysis reveals fundamental structural differences between cryptocurrency markets (Bitcoin[Nak08]) and traditional markets (EUR/USD, AAPL, QQQ). Bitcoin exhibits distinct “trendy” regime-switching behavior with extreme asymmetry (75% bear days, 25% bull days) and excellent cluster separation (silhouette score 0.83), while all tra- ditional markets show balanced, symmetric mean-reverting characteristics ( 50/50 split) with moderate cluster separation (0.55-0.59). Volatility follows a clear hierarchy: Bitcoin (1.05%) ≫ Equities (0.35-0.40%) > Forex (0.18%). 1. Introduction Understanding market regime dynamics is crucial for algorithmic trading strategies. While previous work has characterized individual markets, little research has systematically com- pared regime structures across asset classes using unsupervised learning techniques. This study addresses this gap by applying UMAP (Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection) di- mensionality reduction followed by K-means clustering to daily intraday patterns across cryp- tocurrency, forex, and equity markets. Our key finding is that the “trendy” characterization often applied broadly to financial mar- kets is, in fact, crypto-specific. Traditional markets (both stocks and forex) share remarkably similar structural properties despite their different underlying mechanisms, while cryptocur- rency markets exhibit fundamentally different dynamics. 2. Methodology 2.1. Data Processing. We analyzed four major assets over the period 2021-01-01 to 2024-12- 31: • Bitcoin (BTC) : X:BTCUSD, 1,460 complete days (24/7 trading) • Euro/US Dollar (EUR/USD) : C:EURUSD, 889 complete days (excludes weekends) • Apple Inc. (AAPL) : 998 complete days (NYSE hours 9:30-16:00 ET) • Invesco QQQ Trust (QQQ) : 998 complete days (NYSE hours 9:30-16:00 ET) For each asset, we: (1) Obtained 15-minute bar data (2) Constructed daily vectors by normalizing each day to percentage changes from the opening price (3) For BTC (24/7): 96-dimensional vectors (96 15-minute intervals) (4) For EUR/USD (24/5): 96-dimensional vectors (filtered for 24-hour trading) (5) For stocks: 26-dimensional vectors (6.5 hours NYSE trading / 15 min = 26 intervals) 2.2. Our backtest results show that an 8-month rolling window (240-day lookback) achieves +5.29% cumulative return vs -7.65% for a 4-month window, an advantage of +12.94 percentage points. This validates our UMAP findings: (1) Regime Recognition: Longer history helps identify which cluster (bull/bear) is cur- rently active (2) Asymmetry Learning: Model needs extensive data to learn the 75/25 split and asym- metric returns (3) Regime Transitions: 8 months captures 90 regime changes, teaching transition pat- terns (4) Year Variability: Longer window captures different market characters (2021 vs 2023 mix) 5.2. Traditional Markets Require Different Approaches. For EUR/USD, AAPL, and QQQ: (1) Stable Structure: 50/50 split is constant across years (2) Symmetric Returns: No complex asymmetry to learn (3) Shorter Regimes: 2-day median persistence fits well in shorter windows (4) Lower Volatility: Smaller moves require less historical context Similar strategies could work across these assets given their structural similarities. The emphasis should be on technical mean-reversion rather than regime detection. 6. Conclusions This study reveals fundamental structural differences between cryptocurrency and traditional markets: (1) Crypto markets (BTC) are fundamentally different from ALL traditional markets: Higher volatility (3-6 × ), asymmetric regime structure (75% bear, 25% bull vs 50/50), extreme returns in bull regime (+3.62% avg vs +0.33-0.87%), variable market character year-to-year vs stable, and much higher cluster separation (0.83 vs 0.55-0.59). (2) The “trendy” characterization is CRYPTO-SPECIFIC: BTC shows clear regime- switching with extended bear runs (25 days max). ALL traditional markets (EUR/USD, AAPL, QQQ) are balanced, symmetric, and mean-reverting. Stocks show intermediate volatility between BTC and forex, closer to forex, however. (3) Traditional markets likely require different modeling approaches: Shorter look- back may suffice given stable 50/50 structure. Focus should be on technical mean- reversion rather than regime detection. Similar strategies could work across EUR/USD, AAPL, QQQ given their structural similarities. These findings have important implications for algorithmic trading strategy design: cryp- tocurrency markets require sophisticated regime detection with extended lookback periods, while traditional markets benefit from simpler mean-reversion approaches with shorter win- dows.
